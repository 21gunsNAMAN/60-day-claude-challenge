Executive Summary

FlowAI is a B2B SaaS platform that uses generative AI to automate repetitive workflows for small and mid‑sized teams (5–200 employees). The product connects to existing tools (Slack, Gmail, Notion, Jira, etc.) and lets users create “AI agents” that handle tasks like meeting summarisation, lead enrichment, ticket triage, and document drafting — without writing code.

📌 Startup at a Glance

  • Customer: SMBs with 5–200 employees, especially operations, sales, and product teams
  • MVP: No‑code AI agent builder + 10 pre‑built workflow templates
  • Value Prop: Save 10+ hours/team/week with zero engineering effort
  • Pricing: $49/seat/month (annual) or $59/seat/month (monthly)
  • Revenue Model: 100% recurring subscription (SaaS)
  • GTM: Product‑led growth + outbound SDRs + partner ecosystem

⚡ Key Metrics (Year 1 Target)

  • ARR: $1.2M
  • Customers: 85
  • Avg. Deal Size: $14,100
  • Gross Margin: 78%
  • CAC: $3,400
  • LTV: $28,200   (LTV:CAC = 8.3×)
🟢 Verdict: FlowAI is investable. Strong unit economics, clear customer pain, and a distribution‑first GTM plan. The biggest risk is AI model cost scaling, which is hedged by tiered pricing and usage caps.

Competitive Position & Moat

⚔️ Competitor Landscape

  • Zapier / Make: Broad integrations but no AI‑native workflows
  • OpenAI Assistants: Powerful but require engineering to deploy
  • Notion AI / Slack GPT: Embedded features, not standalone
  • Microsoft Copilot: Enterprise‑only, expensive, complex
  • FlowAI differentiator: No‑code + pre‑built templates + SMB pricing

🏰 Moat (Defensibility)

  • Data flywheel: Proprietary workflow library improves with usage
  • Integration depth: 25+ pre‑built connectors
  • Template network effect: Users share & remix workflows
  • Switching cost: 3‑month setup time to recreate workflows
  • Brand: “The easiest AI workflow tool for SMBs”

Reverse SWOT Analysis

💪 Strengths (Internal)

  • Strong unit economics (LTV:CAC = 8.3×)
  • No‑code product reduces sales friction
  • Founder team has AI + SaaS ops experience
  • YC alumni network provides warm leads

⚠️ Weaknesses (Internal)

  • AI model costs are variable & unpredictable
  • Early brand awareness is low
  • Limited sales capacity (2 SDRs initially)
  • Product still maturing (v1.0)

🌱 Opportunities (External)

  • Huge demand for AI automation in SMBs
  • Partnerships with Slack, Notion, Jira
  • Vertical‑specific templates (legal, real estate)
  • Potential for API marketplace

🔥 Threats (External)

  • Big tech (Microsoft, Google) entering SMB AI
  • Commoditisation of base AI models
  • Privacy regulations around AI data
  • Economic downturn reducing software spend
