# Day 3 – Role‑Based Prompting

## 🧠 What is Role‑Based Prompting?

**Role‑Based Prompting** means telling Claude *who to be* before you ask your question.  
Instead of a generic “How do I…?” you say:  
> *“You are a **seasoned startup founder**. How do I validate a new app idea with no budget?”*

Claude then answers not as a general AI, but as if it had years of founder experience – with vocabulary, priorities, and examples that fit that role.

### Why does it matter?
- Claude is a **generalist by default** – safe, broad, and sometimes shallow.  
- A **role** filters its knowledge through a specific lens → more relevant, actionable, and professional answers.

### How a role changes the response
| Without a role | With a role |
|----------------|--------------|
| “Define your audience, build a plan…” (generic) | “First, talk to 10 potential users. Here’s a lean canvas template…” (founder‑specific) |
| Neutral tone | Tone of a domain expert (lawyer, marketer, developer) |
| One‑size‑fits‑all advice | Tailored vocabulary and depth |

✅ With a role prompt (Founder)
text
You are an experienced startup founder who has launched three B2B SaaS products.  
I'm launching a new API management platform next quarter targeting mid‑size tech companies.  
What should my 30‑day pre‑launch plan look like?
Expert‑level answer (excerpt):

“Week 1: finalise your API documentation and create a public sandbox.
Week 2: invite 20‑30 developers to a private beta – give them free credits in exchange for feedback.
Week 3: publish a ‘coming soon’ page with a waitlist and technical deep‑dive blog posts.
Week 4: run a live demo webinar and open early access to the first 100 signups.”

✨ Three Practical Benefits
Saves time – one role sentence replaces paragraphs of context.

Unlocks specialist knowledge – Claude can act as a lawyer, CTO, HR manager, or marketer instantly.

Produces ready‑to‑use outputs – less editing, more copy‑paste value.
